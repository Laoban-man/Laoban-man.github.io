Kidney glomerus segmentation (WIP)

Detecting functional tissue units in kidney tissue samples

The Human BioMolecular Atlas Program (HuBMAP) is designed to map the human body at the level of functional tissue units to better understand the the relationship and interactions between cells and tissues.

The dataset is comprised of few but very large (>500MB - 5GB) image files. Beyond the instance segmentation challenge, the significant size of individual images will require pre-processing in order to achieve manageable dimensions for a laptop. To improve training opportunities, data augmentation will be conducted to expand the training data by altering original images and due to the size of the original images, creating new smaller images. Image annotations will be converted to relevant formats and adapted to the smaller images. The new data will then be used to train Detectron2 Mask R-CNN networks on instance segmentation tasks.
